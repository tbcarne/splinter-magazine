About Splinter

Who are we?

Morpheus told Neo in The Matrix that 'there's something wrong with the world. You don't know what it is, but it's there, like a splinter in your mind, driving you mad.' We operate under the firm belief that creative iconoclasts, holding splinters in their mind, continue to change the world.

Splinter is a space for those people. A peer-judged platform for long-form writing — where the best ideas surface through the community, not through an editor's taste.

No other social platform exists purely as a collaborative thought space, where creators and consumers alike can review and engage in genuine discourse.

Trust Is Everything

We are operating on a trust-based system. Don't abuse that trust. The only rules of Splinter ('the Journal') are:

  • 1. Must be original work and previously unpublished. By submitting content, you warrant that you are the original author or have obtained all necessary permissions for publication.
  • 2. The Journal reserves the right to review, edit, or reject any submission at its sole discretion.
  • 3. You are solely responsible for your submission. The Journal accepts no liability for any loss or damage arising from the publication of your submission, to the extent permitted by law.
  • 4. Content must not:
    • a.Contain defamatory, obscene, offensive, abusive, or otherwise unlawful content; or
    • b.Contain hate speech, inciting violence, or promoting discrimination based on race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected attribute; or
    • c.Infringe the intellectual property rights, privacy, or other rights of any third party; or
    • d.Contain threats, harassment, or bullying; or
    • e.Violate any applicable law, including but not limited to the Racial Discrimination Act 1975 (Cth), Sex Discrimination Act 1984 (Cth), Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (Cth), and relevant state/territory anti-discrimination laws.
